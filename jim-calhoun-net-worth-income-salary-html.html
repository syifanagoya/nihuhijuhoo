James A. Calhoun, a former collegiate basketball coach, was born on May 10, 1942, in Braintree, Massachusetts, USA. He coached Northeastern University from 1972 to 1986 and Connecticut from 1986 to 2012, winning three NCAA titles (1999, 2004, and 2011) and compiling an 873–380 (.697) record. In 2005, he was elected to the Basketball Hall of Fame.
